Project Title: Sound for Silence
Subtitle: A Comprehensive Digital Ecosystem for Cognitive Development and Therapeutic Monitoring

1. Project Overview
Sound for Silence is a multi-platform healthcare solution designed to support children undergoing cognitive development therapy (such as speech, auditory, or behavioral therapy). The project addresses the need for structured, "anytime-anywhere" learning by providing a curated educational environment for children while giving therapists a powerful web-based dashboard to monitor progress, analyze quiz data, and record clinical assessments.

2. System Architecture
The system is divided into two primary interfaces:

The Mobile Application (Patient/Parent Portal): A gamified learning environment where children consume therapeutic content and take quizzes to reinforce learning.

The Web Dashboard (Therapist Portal): A centralized management system for therapists to track patient lists, review objective quiz performance, and input subjective clinical scores.

3. Key Features
A. Gamified Learning & Progression (Mobile App)
Structured Learning Stages: Content is organized into "Learning Stages" to ensure a logical developmental path.

Sequential Video Access: To ensure mastery, the system enforces a "Prerequisite Logic"—a child must complete the current video before the next one in the stage is unlocked.

Interactive Quizzes: Each video is followed by a quiz to measure immediate retention and cognitive response.

Progress Tracking: Real-time dashboards for parents to monitor day streaks, video completion percentages, and overall progress.

B. Clinical Monitoring & Assessment (Web Portal)
Assigned Patient Management: Therapists can view a list of all children under their care, including critical data like implant dates (useful for cochlear implant or medical device tracking) and "Next Due" assessment dates.

Objective Data Review: Therapists can pull up "Child Quiz Scores" to see how the child performed on app-based tasks (Pass/Fail status and percentage).

Subjective Clinical Scoring: Based on quiz performance and clinical observation, therapists record two primary metrics:

CAP Score (Category of Auditory Perception): Ranging from 0–7.

SIR Score (Speech Intelligibility Rating): Ranging from 0–5.

Assessment History: A longitudinal view of the child’s scores over 3-month, 6-month, or 12-month periods to visualize growth.

5. Clinical Impact
By digitizing the therapy process, Sound for Silence ensures that therapy does not stop at the clinic doors. It provides therapists with data-driven insights rather than relying solely on parent anecdotes, leading to more accurate clinical adjustments and better developmental outcomes for the child.
